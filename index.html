New Career. Who dis?

After listening to a few too many NPR episodes about the need for women in STEM fields, reading too much about the growing need for coders, and having said far too many times, "I wish there was an app that..." I enrolled in a coding bootcamp with We Can {Code} IT in Cleveland, Ohio.

It didn't take me long to realize that software development and web design perfectly synthesized my skills and interests - it's an ever-changing field, where I constantly have to learn, adapt, solve problems, operate creatively and logically - all while making something that fills a need, solves a problem, or makes something easier.

I studied art in college, and I always think back on a professor I had who would always introduce an assignment this way, "We have a problem to solve." I hated it at the time, but now I understand what he meant - how am I going to translate a concept into an image, and how will people translate my image? I see coding the same way - a translatation of concepts made available to anyone.
